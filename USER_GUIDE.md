## Price Lookup

The fastest way to check market prices for any card.

### Quick Lookup (Camera)

1. Tap **Price Lookup** tab at the bottom
2. Point your camera at the card
3. The app automatically recognizes the card
4. See instant market pricing:
   - **Market Value**: Current average price
   - **Low**: Recent low sale
   - **High**: Recent high sale
   - **TCGPlayer**: Price from TCGPlayer (if available)

**Tips**:
- Ensure good lighting for best results
- Keep the card flat and centered
- The card name and set should be clearly visible

### Manual Search

If camera lookup doesn't work or you prefer to search:

1. Tap **Price Lookup** tab
2. Tap the **Search** bar at the top
3. Type the card name (e.g., "Charizard VMAX")
4. Select the correct card from results
5. View pricing details

### Understanding Price Data

**Market Value**: Average of recent sales (last 30 days)
**Low/High**: Price range from recent sales
**Last Updated**: When pricing data was refreshed

**Note**: Prices refresh every 24 hours automatically. Pull down to refresh manually.

### Adding to Inventory

From any price lookup result:

1. Tap **Add to Inventory** button
2. Enter:
   - **Quantity**: How many copies you have
   - **Purchase Cost**: What you paid (optional but recommended)
   - **Condition**: Near Mint, Lightly Played, etc.
3. Tap **Save**

The card is now in your inventory with automatic profit tracking!

### Profit Tracking

CardShow Pro automatically calculates profit for each card:

**Profit = Market Value - Purchase Cost**

**Profit Margin % = (Market Value - Purchase Cost) / Purchase Cost Ã— 100**

**Example**:

## Sales Tracking

Record sales and track platform fees for accurate profit calculations.

### Recording a Sale

1. Go to **Tools** tab â†’ **Sales Tracker**
2. Tap **+ New Sale**
3. Select the card sold (from your inventory)
4. Enter sale details:
   - **Sale Price**: What the buyer paid
   - **Platform**: Where you sold it (eBay, TCGPlayer, In-Person, etc.)
   - **Date**: When the sale occurred (defaults to today)
   - **Notes**: Optional (buyer name, event name, etc.)
5. Tap **Save**

### Platform Fee Calculations

CardShow Pro automatically calculates platform fees based on where you sold:

**eBay**:
- Selling fee: 12.9% + $0.30
- Payment processing: 3.49% + $0.49
- **Total fees**: ~16.4%

**TCGPlayer**:
- Direct: 10.25% + $0.50
- Marketplace: 12.5% + $0.50

**In-Person / Card Show**:
- No fees (100% profit)

**Custom**:
- Enter your own fee percentage

### Understanding Net Profit

For each sale, you'll see:

- **Sale Price**: What the buyer paid
- **Platform Fees**: Automatically calculated
- **Purchase Cost**: What you originally paid
- **Net Profit**: Sale Price - Fees - Purchase Cost

**Example (eBay Sale)**:
- Sale Price: $100.00
- Platform Fees: -$16.40 (16.4%)
- Purchase Cost: -$50.00
- **Net Profit: $33.60**

## Trade Analyzer

Evaluate trades with customizable percentage values for each card.

### How It Works

The Trade Analyzer helps you determine if a trade is fair by comparing the total value of cards you're giving vs. cards you're receiving.

You can set custom percentages for each card (e.g., value a Charizard at 80% market price because it's harder to sell).

### Creating a Trade Analysis

1. Go to **Tools** â†’ **Trade Analyzer**
2. Tap **+ New Trade**
3. **Add Cards You're Giving**:
   - Tap **+ Add Card**
   - Search for card
   - Set **Percentage** (default: 80%)
     - 80% = value at 80% of market price
     - 100% = value at full market price
     - 50% = value at half market price
   - Repeat for all cards you're giving
4. **Add Cards You're Receiving**:
   - Same process as above
   - Set percentage for each received card
5. Tap **Calculate**

### Reading the Results

You'll see:

**Your Side**:
- Cards you're giving
- Market value per card
- Adjusted value (market Ã— percentage)
- **Total Value**: Sum of all adjusted values

**Their Side**:
- Cards you're receiving
- Market value per card
- Adjusted value (market Ã— percentage)
- **Total Value**: Sum of all adjusted values

**Trade Result**:
- **Green** = Good trade (you're getting more value)
- **Red** = Bad trade (you're giving more value)
- **Yellow** = Fair trade (values are close)

**Difference**: How much you're winning/losing in the trade

### Setting Custom Percentages

Why would you value a card below 100%?

- **Slow movers**: Cards that sit in inventory (value at 70-80%)
- **Condition**: Damaged cards (value at 40-60%)
- **Demand**: Cards you know sell quickly (value at 90-100%)
- **Personal preference**: Cards you want (value at 100%+)

**Example**:
- Charizard VMAX (market: $100) at 80% = $80 trade value
- You're less confident you'll sell it quickly, so you value it conservatively

### Saving Trade Analyses

1. After calculating, tap **Save Trade**
2. Add a name (e.g., "Trade with John at Card Show")
3. View saved trades in **Trade History**

This helps you track what trades you've analyzed and reference them later.
